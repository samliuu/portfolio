Project Overview

An interactive light installation that gave students an immersive space for meditation and mindfulness on campus by using animated colors to display visual representations of emotions, with the aim of giving students a place of restoration and release. By introducing color therapy in the form of animated lights, students could exist in a restorative space illuminated by their manifestations of emotions.

The space was constructed with LED lights diffused by cotton balls to create a soft and inviting glow. The lights were animated based on predetermined designs that involve both color and movement to convey the desired emotion.

Lighten Up took take place in an Ell Hall classroom during the last half of the month of March, targeting Northeastern's Wellness Week in collaboration with Northeastern's Sacred Space.

Background

Our overall goal was to create an interactive light installation by the end of the school year with the goal of giving students an immersive space for meditation, mindfulness, and relaxation on campus. We conducted a survey with Northeastern students asking about their experiences with mindfulness and meditation on campus and identified a need for a space like this to exist and be easily accessible to students. We wanted to find a place where students can go to have some visual release of anything they struggle with, and have interactive lights and colors meet their therapeutic needs that they specified through entering on a website

    • Research

    Our research process was extremely exploratory. We kept broad guidelines for the idea of our project -- an interactive experience that involved animated lights -- but kept our minds open throughout the semester to how that might form. We brainstormed different ideas through class activities, conducted user research and competitive analysis, sketched ideas and worked through personas.

    We discovered that we wanted to explore color theory and looked into how color maps and human emotion. With basic understanding of human emotion and color theory, we ultimately decided to focus our project to serve the needs of stressed students.

    Defining the MVP

    We identified the following key features:

    • A functioning, high-fidelity prototype of user interface
    • Designed animations
    • Solidified installation space
    • Fully constructed installation

    After thinking through possible use cases and considering the typical actions and relaxation patterns of students, we decided some core functionality of our space:

    • Partnering with the Sacred Space will provide legitamcy and raise awareness for our installation. With their help, we can get funding, market the project, and recieve incremental design feedback from who work daily with student mental health and restorative practices.
    • Interaction with the sacred space should be easy to access. We decided that having a mobile accessible web application would be the best way for students to interact with the sapce. As almost every student has their own phone, it will easy for them to pull their phone out upon entry and access the site.
    • Lighten Up has a default state. When no one is actively interacting with the installation, there should be a default animation and light show that happens. This will be very minimal and provide neutral emotional benefits.
    • Lighten Up is not meant to 'cure'. We want to market this space as a restorative experience, but it is not intended to heal or force feelings upon someone. Color therapy is rooted in mental acceptance. The color animations are designed to reflect feelings and thoughts in order to promote ease of mind and distract from negative feelings.

    Raspberry Pi & Arduino Setup

    The Raspberry Pi is like a small computer that can control the LEDs. We wrote a small script for each of the animations and we used our server to connect with the Raspberry Pi to play those animations when the user submits the survey form. We used the Arduino software application to practice our LED animations. This was where we sampled our animations and worked through any small bugs before we began development with the actual equipment.


    Constructing the Installation

    The installation was located in the Northeastern Center for Spirituality, Dialogue, and Service (Ell Hall 206F). In the space, we had the LEDs on the wall with stuffing to dim the lights.
