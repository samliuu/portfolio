Project Overview

An interactive light installation that gave students an immersive space for meditation and mindfulness on campus by using animated colors to display visual representations of emotions, with the aim of giving students a place of restoration and release. By introducing color therapy in the form of animated lights, students could exist in a restorative space illuminated by their manifestations of emotions.

The space was constructed with LED lights diffused by cotton balls to create a soft and inviting glow. The lights were animated based on predetermined designs that involve both color and movement to convey the desired emotion.

Lighten Up took take place in an Ell Hall classroom during the last half of the month of March, targeting Northeastern's Wellness Week in collaboration with Northeastern's Sacred Space.

Background

Our overall goal was to create an interactive light installation by the end of the school year with the goal of giving students an immersive space for meditation, mindfulness, and relaxation on campus. We conducted a survey with Northeastern students asking about their experiences with mindfulness and meditation on campus and identified a need for a space like this to exist and be easily accessible to students. We wanted to find a place where students can go to have some visual release of anything they struggle with, and have interactive lights and colors meet their therapeutic needs that they specified through entering on a website

    • Research

    Our research process was extremely exploratory. We kept broad guidelines for the idea of our project -- an interactive experience that involved animated lights -- but kept our minds open throughout the semester to how that might form. We brainstormed different ideas through class activities, conducted user research and competitive analysis, sketched ideas and worked through personas. Through this process, we discovered that we wanted to explore color theory and looked into how color maps and human emotion. With basic understanding of human emotion and color theory, we ultimately decided to focus our project to serve the needs of stressed students.

    Defining the MVP

    We identified the following key features:

    • A functioning, high-fidelity prototype of user interface
    • Designed animations
    • Solidified installation space
    • Fully constructed installation

    After thinking through possible use cases and considering the typical actions and relaxation patterns of students, we decided some core functionality of our space:

    • Partnering with the Sacred Space will provide legitamcy and raise awareness for our installation. With their help, we got funding, marketed the project, and recieved incremental design feedback from who worked daily with student mental health and restorative practices.
    • Lighten Up has a default state. When no one was actively interacting with the installation, there was a default animation and light show that happened. This was very minimal and provided neutral emotional benefits.
    • Lighten Up was not meant to 'cure'. We wanted to market this space as a restorative experience, but it was not intended to heal or force feelings upon someone. The color animations were designed to reflect feelings and thoughts in order to promote ease of mind and distract from negative feelings.

    Home Page

    The home screen gave the user a short overview of the purpose of installation. From the home screen, users could access the survey by pressing the 'Enter the restorative space>' to interact with the LED lights. Users can also interact with the 'Learn more>' button to access the color theory research page.


    Survey Screens

    To interact with the installation, users had to complete a quick survey of: their current feeling, a feeling they want to let go of, and a feeling they want to manifest. Depending on the user input, a color based off of what we researched appear in the installation. Once the user submitted their survey, they were directed to the confirmation screen and their manifested feeling would be added to the queue. The confirmation screen showed the user the manifested color and a preview of what kinds of colors they were able to see in the installation.



    Color Theory Screen

    We displayed a color palette for different types of feelings based on the research we have done. There are 5 palettes that were combined and mapped to different feelings. These colors correlated to an animation that was lit up through LED lights and fiber optics in the installation. We also included the sources to the research so that users can look into color theory.

    Raspberry Pi & Arduino Setup

    The Raspberry Pi is similar to a small computer that can control the LEDs. We wrote a small script for each of the animations and we used our server to connect with the Raspberry Pi to play those animations when the user submitted the survey form. We used the Arduino software application to practice our LED animations. This was where we sampled our animations and worked through any small bugs before we began development with the actual equipment.


    Constructing the Installation

    The installation was located in the Northeastern Center for Spirituality, Dialogue, and Service (Ell Hall 206F). In the space, we had the LEDs on the wall with stuffing to dim the lights.
