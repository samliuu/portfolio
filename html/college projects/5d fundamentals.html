Course Description

In this course, I explored the language of interactive experience as a compelling way communicate meaning. It also considers the factors in the enviornment and how they can change the way humans experience things. Drawing was incorporated into this course to understand the present and future experiences.

Timeline

For my fictional design project, I decided to focus on the values of delivery services -- Uber Eats, Door Dash, GrubHub, and Postmates -- and reflect on the kinds of values that are embedded in these services. I created the iCooker to engage my audience through humor and wonder.

Artist Statement

The iCooker is a combined burner stove top and cutting board with several automatic kitchen utensils and a control panel to manage the iCooker, which is all powered through a phone that creates electricity when plugged into the phone port. The iCooker is like a small mini kitchen that is portable and can allow users to conveniently cook anywhere they want. One of the main purposes of the iCooker is to get people off their phones and have them start focusing on their daily tasks, like cooking. Another purpose of the iCooker is to promote healthier lifestyles and diets for those who have little experience with cooking and those who often order take out for their meals. Also, the iCooker informs the user about how their meals are affecting them, for instance, the control panel shows the user how many calories are being consumed, tracks how many nutrients the user is intaking, and notifies the user of how much exercise is needed to burn off the meal. The only thing that the user needs to provide are the ingredients to a recipe.


There are two events that inspired the creation of the iCooker: my college roommate and the project, ​Collaborative Cooking​, created by Christian Isberg, Petter Johansson, Lasse Korsgaard, and Carl Berglöf. My college roommate inspired the iCooker because he spends most of his time watching television and playing video games and he often orders take out because he has little to no experience with cooking. The ​Collaborative Cooking​ project also inspired the iCooker because it is a digital platform that influences and interacts with a physical cooking machine to explore how people will prepare and discuss food. This project enhances the way people interact with food, which could also be the case with the iCooker, since beginner cooks will potentially use this product. One of my goals for creating the iCooker is to keep people off their phones for a couple of hours a day because people are constantly looking at and scrolling through their phones. The phone port will force people to stop using their phones while they are cooking. Another goal is to allow people to cook with no distractions.

Storyboard

The storyboard follows Arwin, who has little to no experience with cooking. Arwin is very hungry, but it is too late to order food. He attempts to cook, but ends up in himself in the process. He asks for advice online and goes to sleep hungry. In the morning, he stumbles upon a response that recommened the iCooker, an automatic burner and cutting board that helps people cook. Arwin immediately orders the iCooker and it arrives the next day. He decides to make pan fried noodles. To turn on the iCooker, Arwin plugs in his phone into the port and the device powers on. Arwin places washed vegetables on the cutting board section of the iCooker and a mechanical arm with a knife pops out to start cutting the vegetables. Then, he places a pan with water on the burner. The control pannel gives Arwin instructions on how to cook the noodles and vegetables. 10 minutes later, the meal is ready.

Artist Statement

This Chinese New Year Celebration event called ‘The Three Lions’ is located in Boston, Chinatown and it features a magenta, sky blue, and orange lion dance costume ceramic statues that are placed on a dark red platform, which is also located next to a traditional Chinese lion dance drum, a Chinese gong, and five boxes with lion dance costumes made out of plastic and fabric. The statues are made out of ceramic glass because it is tough, translucent, and opalescent which will bring out the bright colors. Traditional lion dance costumes are typically made out of wood and paper, which could be troublesome when children interact with it. To make the costumes more durable, the tail of the body is made out of fabric and the head of the dragon is made out of plastic and fabric. Traditional instruments are also used because it will give everyone the full experience of the whole lion dance.


An experience that inspired this creation was going to the Chinese New Year festivals in Boston, Chinatown to see the powerful and mesmerizing lion performance as a child. I remember the vivid colors of the lion's costume and the lion's fluttering eyes, but the only thing I wanted to do was to touch and be inside of the lion's head. Since the material of the lion costumes was delicate, I was only able to view from afar, but did not have the experience of interacting with the lion costume. An event that inspired this creation was the seasonal event, Films at the Gate presented by the Asian Community Development Corporation (ACDC). Every summer in Boston, the Chinatown and ACDC community members create a free outdoor theater showing classic Chinese-language and Kung-Fu films on the Rose Kennedy Greenway - Chinatown Park. This seasonal event allowed people around the community to come together and enjoy their time together, which is one of my goals when I was creating this project. Another one of my goals was to allow children and adults to have the experience to interact with the lion dance costumes and learn how to do some of the basic dances.
