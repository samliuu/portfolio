Typography 1



Course Description

Typography 1 offers an introduction to type as a designed form and typographic practices that are important for formal communication. The course emphasizes all the basic elements and tools used in typographic hiearchy and page design such as sequence, navigation, legibility, graphic punctuation, font, weight, size, spacing, and composition.

My Neighbor Totoro Play Book

The purpose of this project was to learn about book design. The requirements were to present text by displaying the formal aspects of typography and page design. I also had to explore sequencing and rhythm to express the narrative of the story.


Book Plates

I created 24 compositions that work together as a series while incorporating each of the 24 typefaces used in my logotypes. This allowed me to define parameters, to explore range of variation, to reinforce sequence and repetition.

For my first draft, I experimented with 10 colors, 3 sequences, and only used a black background. My bookcover used all the colors and used one sequence. After a couple of iterations, my final draft still used the same amount of colors. The changes I made were to include the typefaces in my logotypes and add more sequences for more variation.






Logotype Book

I was introduced to 24 typefaces and I learned to recognize the difference between what is successful and unsuccessful in the architecture of character design while sharpening my technical skills. I created a mark by combining two letter forms from the same font family and the design of the mark has to show an understanding between strokes and intersections. Each section has background on the designer who created the type, when the typeface was created, and how it was used.
